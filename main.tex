\title{Two Squeezed Cells per Heisenberg Cell}

\begin{abstract}
Heisenberg bounds a quantum state's action in position and momentum, and the same $\hbar$ binds de Gosson's pair of inner scales, the sharpest position and momentum the state can resolve. The Wigner function sampled finer than these goes negative, not because the formalism fails but because the question is forbidden. Resolved at the scale the state itself permits, it is non-negative everywhere, with all interference structure including Zurek's sub-Planck chessboard appearing as positive density.
\end{abstract}

% ======================================================================
% SLICE 1 --- PREMISE
% ======================================================================

A quantum state cannot be a point in phase space. The Heisenberg and Robertson--Schr\"odinger uncertainty principles \cite{robertson1929,schrodinger1930},
\begin{equation}
\sigma_q \sigma_p \ge \hbar/2,
\label{eq:hup}
\end{equation}
forbid it. Zero area is incompatible with finite uncertainty.

\begin{figure}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/quantum_of_action.pdf}
\caption{\textbf{Conjugate structure of the quantum state} (schematic, 1D case). Heisenberg admits semi-axes $\Delta q = \sqrt{2}\sigma_q$, $\Delta p = \sqrt{2}\sigma_p$, and the state's $1\sigma$ contour is its Fermi blob of area $A = \pi\Delta q\Delta p \ge h/2$. Inside sit two squeezed cells, $a_q = \pi\,\delta q\,\Delta p = h/2$ squeezed along position and $a_p = \pi\,\Delta q\,\delta p = h/2$ squeezed along momentum, with widths $\delta q = \hbar/\Delta p$ and $\delta p = \hbar/\Delta q$. As $A$ grows, $\Delta p$ and $\Delta q$ grow while $\delta q$ and $\delta p$ shrink. The same $\hbar$ binds the outer extent and the inner resolution.}
\label{fig:quantum_of_action}
\end{figure}

The state's extent in phase space is its Fermi blob \cite{degosson2013fermiblobs}, hereafter Heisenberg cell, the elliptical region defined by its covariance with semi-axes $\Delta q = \sqrt{2}\sigma_q$ and $\Delta p = \sqrt{2}\sigma_p$ and area
\begin{equation}
A = \pi \Delta q \Delta p \ge h/2,
\label{eq:fermi_blob_area}
\end{equation}
saturating only for the harmonic ground state. Its interior is partitioned by symplectic polar duality \cite{degossoncharlyne2022} into two quantum blobs \cite{degosson2013qblobs}, hereafter squeezed cells,
\begin{equation}
a_q = \pi\,\delta q\,\Delta p = h/2, \qquad a_p = \pi\,\Delta q\,\delta p = h/2,
\label{eq:quantum_blobs}
\end{equation}
each of area exactly $h/2$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:quantum_of_action}). The squeezed widths $\delta q, \delta p$ are the polar duals of the anti-squeezed widths $\Delta p, \Delta q$ fixed by the same $\hbar$ as in Eq.~(\ref{eq:hup}),
\begin{equation}
\delta q = \hbar/\Delta p, \qquad \delta p = \hbar/\Delta q.
\label{eq:reciprocal}
\end{equation}
 A phase-space ellipsoid is a squeezed cell if and only if its position-space projection and momentum-space intersection are polar duals \cite{degossoncharlyne2022}. Gromov's non-squeezing theorem \cite{gromov1985} forbids any canonical transformation from compressing a squeezed cell below $h/2$. The pairing is exact and proven for arbitrary dimension \cite{degossoncharlyne2022}.

% ======================================================================
% SLICE 2 --- WRONG QUESTION + STATE-APPARATUS PRINCIPLE
% ======================================================================

The Wigner--Weyl distribution \cite{wigner1932}
\begin{equation}
W(q,p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\int \psi^*(q+y)\,\psi(q-y)\,e^{2ipy/\hbar}\, dy
\label{eq:wigner}
\end{equation}
is well-defined, and its marginals reproduce $|\psi(q)|^2$ and $|\hat\psi(p)|^2$ exactly. What is malformed is the limit $\delta q \to 0$ that asks for density at a point. By Eq.~(\ref{eq:reciprocal}) such a limit demands $\Delta p \to \infty$ and unbounded covariance area. Wigner negativity for any non-Gaussian state \cite{hudson1974} is the formalism reporting that pointwise quasi-probability is incompatible with the state's available action, not a pathology of $W$. The well-posed density question lives at the scale where the state actually lives, namely the squeezed cells.

The squeezed cells of Fig.~\ref{fig:quantum_of_action}, written for diagonal covariance, are
\begin{equation}
G_{\delta q}(q,p) = \frac{1}{\pi}\exp\!\left(-\frac{q^2}{\delta q^2} - \frac{p^2}{\Delta p^2}\right),
\label{eq:Gdq}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
G_{\delta p}(q,p) = \frac{1}{\pi}\exp\!\left(-\frac{q^2}{\Delta q^2} - \frac{p^2}{\delta p^2}\right),
\label{eq:Gdp}
\end{equation}
recovered in general by symplectic rotation. Each is itself a squeezed cell at the $h/2$ floor. By the canonical bijection between Gaussian states and squeezed cells \cite{degossoncharlyne2022}, $G_{\delta q}$ is the Wigner transform of the squeezed coherent state matching the state's covariance, and the polar-dual identity follows from Eq.~(\ref{eq:reciprocal}). The convolved Wigner density
\begin{equation}
P_{\delta q}(q,p) = (W * G_{\delta q})(q,p)
\label{eq:Pdq}
\end{equation}
is non-negative everywhere, since convolution with a quantum-blob kernel sends any Wigner function to a non-negative one \cite{degosson2013qblobs}. $P_{\delta q}(q,p)$ is the probability density of the state at the resolution of its own squeezed cells. The polar-dual partner $P_{\delta p}(q,p) = (W * G_{\delta p})(q,p)$ resolves momentum at $\delta p$ symmetrically.

We compute $P_{\delta q}$ numerically for two classes of states. Figure~\ref{fig:wigner} shows three Schr\"odinger eigenstates with kernel widths $\Delta q = \sqrt{2\langle (q-\langle q\rangle)^2\rangle_{\psi_n}}$ and $\Delta p = \sqrt{2\langle p^2\rangle_{\psi_n}}$. The harmonic ground state collapses to a single Gaussian. The Morse oscillator at $n=8$ has $n+1$ alternating-sign Wigner lobes, and $P_{\delta q}$ preserves the full lobe structure as non-negative density features. The symmetric double-well antisymmetric partner $\psi_1$ has an exact wavefunction node at $q=0$ where the Wigner negativity is deepest, and $P_{\delta q}(q,0)$ is strictly positive throughout, including across the node and through the barrier where the Gaussian tails extend from each well.

% SLICE 4 --- CLOSING
% ======================================================================

Convolution of $W$ with any other kernel does not represent the state. It represents an apparatus. Dragoman's phase-space filtering \cite{dragoman2005} convolves $W$ with the Wigner function of an external measurement device. The Cahill--Glauber $s$-parametrized family \cite{cahillglauber1969} interpolates between operator-ordering and detection-scheme parametrizations \cite{wodkiewicz1984}. The Husimi $Q$-function \cite{husimi1940} convolves $W$ with the harmonic-oscillator ground-state blob, which is the vacuum noise of heterodyne detection \cite{leonhardt1997}. Husimi reports the heterodyne measurement, coinciding with the state's own squeezed cell only at the harmonic ground state. Figures~\ref{fig:wigner} and~\ref{fig:cats} show Husimi (overlaid) smooths the interior interferences. $P_{\delta q}$ tracks the state.

Zurek identified the chessboard widths $\delta q$, $\delta p$ and showed that decoherence sensitivity scales with this sub-Planckian structure \cite{zurek2001}. The same narrow widths were identified geometrically by de Gosson from the squeezed cells \cite{degossoncharlyne2022}. The two routes meet at Eq.~(\ref{eq:reciprocal}), where one $\hbar$ binds outer extent to inner resolution. The chessboard is sub-Planckian only when measured against the macroscopic Heisenberg cell. Geometrically, its widths are precisely the minor axes of the squeezed cells. Rather than violating the Planck scale, this fine structure is strictly native to it. Probed at this natural $h/2$ resolution limit, $P_{\delta q}$ and $P_{\delta p}$ are positive density.
