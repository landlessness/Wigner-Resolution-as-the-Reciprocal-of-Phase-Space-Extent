\title{Conjugate Phase-Space Cells Resolve Caustics and Set the Density of a Quantum State}

\begin{abstract}
A quantum state has finite action in phase space. Heisenberg's uncertainty combined with de Gosson's topology forces this action into two conjugate cells of size $h/2$, one squeezed along position, the other along momentum. WKB caustic divergences are leading-order asymptotic artifacts at classical turning points. The Wigner function carries fine-scale features below the conjugate cells. At the scale of the conjugate cells, the state has a density that is everywhere finite-valued and non-negative.
\end{abstract}

% ======================================================================
% SLICE 1 --- PHYSICS
% ======================================================================

A quantum state has finite action in phase space. The Heisenberg and Robertson--Schr\"odinger uncertainty principles \cite{robertson1929,schrodinger1930},
\begin{equation}
\sigma_q \sigma_p \ge \hbar/2,
\label{eq:hup}
\end{equation}
forbid the state from being a point: zero area is incompatible with finite uncertainty.

\begin{figure}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/quantum_of_action.pdf}
\caption{\textbf{Conjugate structure of the quantum state} (schematic, 1D case). The Heisenberg limit $\sigma_q \sigma_p \ge \hbar/2$ admits semi-axes $\Delta q = \sqrt{2}\sigma_q$, $\Delta p = \sqrt{2}\sigma_p$; the state's $1\sigma$ contour is its Fermi blob, of area $A = \pi\Delta q\Delta p \ge h/2$. Inside sit two conjugate quantum blobs, $a_q = \pi\,\delta q\,\Delta p = h/2$ squeezed along position and $a_p = \pi\,\Delta q\,\delta p = h/2$ squeezed along momentum. Their narrow widths are $\delta q = \hbar/\Delta p$ and $\delta p = \hbar/\Delta q$. As $A$ grows, $\Delta p$ and $\Delta q$ grow while $\delta q$ and $\delta p$ shrink: the same $\hbar$ binds the outer extent and the inner resolution.}
\label{fig:quantum_of_action}
\end{figure}

De Gosson identified the state's extent in phase space as the Fermi blob \cite{degosson2013fermiblobs}, the elliptical region defined by its covariance with semi-axes $\Delta q = \sqrt{2}\sigma_q$ and $\Delta p = \sqrt{2}\sigma_p$. Its covariance area,
\begin{equation}
A = \pi \Delta q \Delta p \ge h/2,
\label{eq:fermi_blob_area}
\end{equation}
is distinct from the system's dynamical action, saturating at $h/2$ only for the exact harmonic ground state. A Fermi blob of covariance area $A > h/2$ is not featureless. Its interior holds two conjugate quantum blobs \cite{degosson2009,degossoncharlyne2022},
\begin{equation}
a_q = \pi\,\delta q\,\Delta p = h/2, \qquad a_p = \pi\,\Delta q\,\delta p = h/2,
\label{eq:quantum_blobs}
\end{equation}
each carrying half the Planck quantum of action (Fig.~\ref{fig:quantum_of_action}). The squeezed axes
\begin{equation}
\delta q = \hbar/\Delta p, \qquad \delta p = \hbar/\Delta q
\label{eq:reciprocal}
\end{equation}
are reciprocal to the Fermi-blob's anti-squeezed axes, fixed by the same Heisenberg constant in Eq.~(\ref{eq:hup}). Equation~(\ref{eq:reciprocal}) is itself an uncertainty relation between conjugate resolutions inside a state of fixed covariance, where $\Delta p$ and $\Delta q$ grow with $A$ while $\delta q$ and $\delta p$ shrink. Gromov's non-squeezing theorem \cite{gromov1985} forbids any canonical transformation from compressing a quantum blob below $h/2$. Planck's quantum of action is the floor of phase space.

% ======================================================================
% SLICE 2 --- FORMALISM PROBLEMS
% ======================================================================

The two formalisms behave differently at this floor. The WKB density \cite{berry1977}
\begin{equation}
|\psi_\text{WKB}(q)|^2 = \frac{N^2}{p(q)}\cos^2\!\left(S(q)/\hbar - \pi/4\right)
\label{eq:wkb}
\end{equation}
diverges at every classical turning point, a leading-order asymptotic artifact patched locally by connection formulas and Airy uniformization \cite{langer1937}. The Wigner--Weyl distribution \cite{wigner1932}
\begin{equation}
W(q,p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \psi^*(q+y)\,\psi(q-y)\,e^{2ipy/\hbar}\, dy
\label{eq:wigner}
\end{equation}
takes negative values for any state beyond the ground state, with structure at sub-cell scales. The Husimi $Q$-function \cite{husimi1940} smooths $W$ with a fixed kernel that erases every scale below it.

Pointwise evaluation at a precise $q$ or an exact $(q,p)$ assumes a zero-area limit ($\delta q \to 0$ or $\delta p \to 0$), which is physically forbidden \cite{dragoman2005}. By Eq.~(\ref{eq:reciprocal}) such a limit demands an infinite conjugate spread ($\Delta p \to \infty$ or $\Delta q \to \infty$) and unbounded covariance area ($A \to \infty$). The conjugate cells are the physical scale at which the density of the state is defined.

% ======================================================================
% SLICE 3 --- PHYSICS
% ======================================================================

We adapt the phase-space filtering of \cite{dragoman2005} with a kernel set by the state's own quantum blobs. The conjugate quantum blobs of Fig.~\ref{fig:quantum_of_action}, as Gaussian filters of phase-space area $h/2$, give
\begin{equation}
G_{\delta q}(q,p) = \frac{1}{\pi}\exp\!\left(-\frac{q^2}{\delta q^2} - \frac{p^2}{\Delta p^2}\right),
\label{eq:Gdq}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
G_{\delta p}(q,p) = \frac{1}{\pi}\exp\!\left(-\frac{q^2}{\Delta q^2} - \frac{p^2}{\delta p^2}\right),
\label{eq:Gdp}
\end{equation}
$G_{\delta q}$ squeezed along position and $G_{\delta p}$ along momentum, written here for diagonal covariance and recovered in general by symplectic rotation. Each kernel is itself a quantum blob at the $h/2$ floor. Gaussian smoothing of the Wigner function with a minimum-uncertainty kernel is the Cahill--Glauber $s$-parametrized construction \cite{cahillglauber1969}, of which Husimi is the fixed-width member, and yields a non-negative distribution \cite{cartwright1976}. de Gosson \cite{degosson2005cellules} connected the symplectic-cell framework to the positivity of Wigner and Husimi functions, fixing the kernel scale at $h/2$. Gromov non-squeezing forbids a narrower kernel; a wider kernel discards resolution the state has.

\label{fig:semiclassical}
\end{figure}

The classical orbit at the Bohr--Sommerfeld energy $E_n$ provides kernel widths $\Delta q = \sqrt{2\langle (q-\langle q\rangle)^2\rangle_\text{orbit}}$ and $\Delta p = \sqrt{2\langle p^2\rangle_\text{orbit}}$. We resolve the oscillating WKB phase-space density $W_\text{WKB}(q,p) \propto \delta(H - E)\,\cos^2(S(q)/\hbar - \pi/4)$, whose $p$-marginal is the diverging $|\psi_\text{WKB}|^2$. The convolved marginal,
\begin{equation}
\rho_{\delta q}(q) = \int (W_\text{WKB} * G_{\delta q})(q,p)\, dp,
\label{eq:rho_marginal}
\end{equation}
is finite at the turning points and strictly positive everywhere due to the kernel's Gaussian tails. Figure~\ref{fig:semiclassical} shows three systems. The harmonic ground state collapses to a single Gaussian. The Morse oscillator at $n=8$ preserves the $n+1$ Bohr--Sommerfeld interference lobes; the WKB caustics become finite peaks. For the symmetric double-well sub-barrier doublet, per-well Langer--Airy constructions place artificial maxima at the inner turning points; $\rho_{\delta q}(q)$ is everywhere finite and seamlessly resolves the physical barrier penetration.

The same kernel applies in the wave formalism, with $\Delta q = \sqrt{2\langle (q-\langle q\rangle)^2\rangle_{\psi_n}}$ and $\Delta p = \sqrt{2\langle p^2\rangle_{\psi_n}}$ from the eigenstate's Robertson--Schr\"odinger covariance. The convolved Wigner density
\begin{equation}
P_{\delta q}(q,p) = (W * G_{\delta q})(q,p)
\label{eq:Pdq}
\end{equation}
is non-negative everywhere, since convolution with a quantum-blob kernel sends any Wigner function to a non-negative one \cite{degosson2013qblobs}. Figure~\ref{fig:wigner} shows the three systems as eigenstates. The Morse row at $n=8$ has $n+1$ alternating-sign Wigner lobes; $P_{\delta q}$ preserves the lobe envelope and is non-negative throughout, while Husimi smooths away the interior interference, leaving only two broad peaks at the classical turning points. The double-well antisymmetric partner $\psi_1$ has an exact wavefunction node at $q=0$, where the Wigner negativity is deepest. At resolution $\delta q$, $P_{\delta q}(q,0)$ is strictly positive throughout, including across the node and through the barrier where the Gaussian tails extend from each well.

Kernel widths from the Robertson--Schr\"odinger covariance of $\psi$ directly. Rows: 2-cat (two lobes), 3-cat (equilateral triangle), 4-cat compass (diagonal lobes), 4-cat Zurek compass (axial lobes). Columns as in Fig.~\ref{fig:wigner}. A cat state has no classical orbit and no Bohr--Sommerfeld action. The Fermi blob is still finite, set by the wavefunction's covariance directly, with $\Delta q = \sqrt{2\langle (q-\langle q\rangle)^2\rangle_\psi}$ and $\Delta p = \sqrt{2\langle p^2\rangle_\psi}$. Figure~\ref{fig:cats} shows four cat configurations. Each row's Wigner cross-section dips below zero, including the central interference fringe of the 2-cat, the pair-fringe envelopes of the 3-cat, and the chessboards of the two 4-cat compasses. Zurek's chessboard width $\delta q = \hbar/\Delta p$ \cite{zurek2001} is sub-Planckian only against the Fermi blob of Eq.~(\ref{eq:fermi_blob_area}); against the conjugate quantum blob $a_q$ of Eq.~(\ref{eq:quantum_blobs}) it is exactly Planckian, saturating $\pi \delta q\,\Delta p = h/2$. $P_{\delta q}$ resolves the chessboard at $\delta q$ rather than smoothing it away, and is non-negative throughout.

% ======================================================================
% SLICE 4 --- CLOSING
% ======================================================================

A quantum state has finite action in phase space and cannot be reduced to a point. Heisenberg's uncertainty and de Gosson's topology fix its outer extent and inner resolution as conjugate scales locked by the same $\hbar$. The WKB density diverges at every classical turning point as a leading-order asymptotic artifact; the Wigner function carries fine-scale features below the conjugate cells. At the scale of the conjugate cells, the state has a density that is everywhere finite-valued and non-negative.
