\title{Wigner Resolution as the Reciprocal of Action Capacity}

\begin{abstract}
The Wigner function of a state with position spread $\Delta x$ and momentum spread $\Delta p$ admits a resolution boundary in phase space set by the state itself. Finer than the sub-Planck scale $\hbar/\Delta p$, the function carries dips below zero. Coarser, the peak structure washes out. At the boundary itself, the same peak structure of locations, spacing, and count reads as a non-negative density. We identify the boundary and the unique Gaussian kernel that resolves at it. The kernel has fixed action $h/2$ and covariance matched to the state, with no free parameters. Truncated Wigner sampling, generalized Husimi smoothing, maximum-likelihood reconstruction, and Cahill--Glauber ordering each succeed at this same scale by different routes, six independent constructions landing on the same boundary in their regimes of success. Wigner resolution is the reciprocal of action.
\end{abstract}

%  OPENING

The Wigner function of a quantum state~\cite{wigner1932} can take negative values~\cite{hudson1974}. Whether it does depends on the resolution at which it is read. We show that this dependence is physical. For any state, there is a single scale in phase space, set by the state's action, above which the Wigner function's peak structure reads as a non-negative density and below which the dips between peaks are exposed. The boundary is the sub-Planck scale $\delta x = \hbar/\Delta p$ identified by Zurek~\cite{zurek2001} and recovered as a polar-dual length in the symplectic construction of de Gosson~\cite{degosson2013qblobs}. It is not a property of the observer or the method. It is a property of the state.

Operationalized, the boundary gives a construction with no free parameters. The unique Gaussian kernel of fixed action $h/2$ matched to the state's covariance, convolved against $W$, produces a non-negative density whose peaks coincide with those of $W$ in location, spacing, and count. The same scale governs every established positive-density construction. Truncated Wigner sampling~\cite{polkovnikov2010}, generalized Husimi smoothing~\cite{husimi1940,wodkiewicz1984}, maximum-likelihood tomography~\cite{lvovsky2009}, and Cahill--Glauber ordering~\cite{cahillglauber1969} each reach it by an independent route. Six constructions landing on the same scale is evidence the scale is physical.

We work in one degree of freedom and align coordinates with the principal axes of the state's covariance ellipse. The Robertson--Schr\"odinger covariance term~\cite{robertson1929,schrodinger1930} vanishes, leaving the position spread $\Delta x = \sqrt{2}\sigma_x$ and the momentum spread $\Delta p = \sqrt{2}\sigma_p$. The symplectic results we use~\cite{degosson2013qblobs,degossoncharlyne2022} extend without modification to higher dimensions and to the full off-diagonal covariance.

The state's covariance defines two phase-space cells (Fig.~\ref{fig:de_gosson_cells}). The extended cell, of action
\begin{equation}
A = \pi\,\Delta x\,\Delta p \ge h/2,
\label{eq:extended_cell}
\end{equation}
is the state's outer extent, floored at the quantum of action by Heisenberg. Inscribed within it is a one-parameter family of squeezed cells of fixed action
\begin{equation}
a = \pi\,\delta_\parallel\,\delta_\perp = h/2
\label{eq:squeezed_cell}
\end{equation}
identified by de Gosson~\cite{degosson2013qblobs,degossoncharlyne2022}. The semi-axes obey the polar-dual identity
\begin{equation}
\delta_\parallel\,\delta_\perp = \hbar.
\label{eq:polar_dual}
\end{equation}
The minor axis is squeezed below the coherent-state scale and the major axis is anti-squeezed above it. Their product is fixed at one quantum of action by Gromov's non-squeezing theorem~\cite{gromov1985}. As the orientation $\theta$ varies,
\begin{equation}
\delta_\parallel(\theta) = \sqrt{\Delta x^2\cos^2\theta + \Delta p^2\sin^2\theta},
\label{eq:delta_parallel}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\delta_\perp(\theta) = \hbar / \delta_\parallel(\theta),
\label{eq:delta_perp}
\end{equation}
where $\theta$ is the rotated quadrature direction of continuous-variable tomography~\cite{vogelrisken1989,lvovsky2009}. At the principal-axis orientations $\theta = 0$ and $\theta = \pi/2$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:zurek_cells}), the minor axes reduce to the sub-Planck scales identified by Zurek~\cite{zurek2001},
\begin{equation}
\delta x = \frac{\hbar}{\Delta p}, \qquad \delta p = \frac{\hbar}{\Delta x}.
\label{eq:zurek}
\end{equation}
The smallest cell the state can locally carry, the spacing of the peaks in its Wigner function, and the boundary at which $W$ changes sign-character are all the same length, set by the action of the state.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE BOUNDARY AND THE KERNEL
% ===============================================================================

The Wigner function of the state,
\begin{equation}
W(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\int \psi^*(x+y)\,\psi(x-y)\,e^{2ipy/\hbar}\,dy,
\label{eq:wigner}
\end{equation}
has peaks spaced by the squeezed scale and dips below zero between consecutive peaks for any state with $A \gg h/2$. The boundary separates these two features. The dips have structure on the squeezed scale. Resolve finer and they are visible. Resolve coarser and the peak structure remains while the dips are erased.

The kernel that resolves at the boundary is the operational form of the boundary itself. It is the Wigner function of the squeezed coherent state matched to the state's covariance,
\begin{equation}
K_\theta(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\exp\!\left(-\frac{x_\theta^2}{\delta_\parallel^2} - \frac{P^2}{\delta_\perp^2}\right),
\label{eq:Gtheta}
\end{equation}
with $x_\theta = x\cos\theta + p\sin\theta$ and $P = -x\sin\theta + p\cos\theta$, of action area $h/2$. The state seen at this resolution is
\begin{equation}
P_\theta(x,p) = (W * K_\theta)(x,p).
\label{eq:Ptheta}
\end{equation}
This is non-negative everywhere. The convolution of two Wigner functions equals the trace inner product of the corresponding density operators~\cite{degosson2005bullsci,degosson2013qblobs}, which is positive for any pair of states. The non-negativity is a standard fact of the de Gosson and quantum-optics literature. What is new is the kernel's uniqueness. Its covariance is fixed by the state, its action is fixed at $h/2$, and its orientation $\theta$ ranges over the inscribed family. This uniqueness is inherited from the boundary having no free parameters either.

The resolution of $P_\theta$ is the full width at half maximum of $K_\theta$ along its minor axis. This is the smallest length scale on which $P_\theta$ varies appreciably. At principal-axis orientation $\theta = \pi/2$ the minor axis is along position with $\delta_\perp = \hbar/\Delta p$, giving
\begin{equation}
\Delta x_{\text{res}} = 2\sqrt{\ln 2}\,\hbar/\Delta p,
\label{eq:resolution}
\end{equation}
the reciprocal of the state's momentum extent up to a numerical factor of order unity. The peaks of $W$ along $x$ appear in $P_\theta$ at the same locations, with the same spacing $\pi\hbar/\Delta p$, and in the same count. What does not appear is the dip below zero between consecutive peaks. That structure lies on the fine side of the boundary.

Figures~\ref{fig:eigen} and~\ref{fig:cats} show $P_{\pi/2}$ for eight states. The library spans squeezed vacuum, harmonic, Morse, and double-well eigenstates, and four cat configurations including Zurek's compass. With the kernel set from each state's covariance, the peaks of $W$ along the chosen cross-section appear in $P_{\pi/2}$ at the same locations, with the same spacing $\pi\hbar/\Delta p$, and in the same count. The negative dips between peaks do not appear. They have structure finer than the kernel resolves. For the compass state, $W(x,0)$ has $N_+$ peaks at fringe spacing $\pi\hbar/\Delta p$ with $N_+ - 1$ negative dips between them. The convolved density $P_{\pi/2}(x,0)$ has the same $N_+$ peaks at the same locations, with peak-to-peak spacing matching the Wigner peak spacing to within the resolution tolerance of Eq.~(\ref{eq:resolution}), and stays non-negative between peaks. Quantitative peak-counting data for all eight states are in the Supplemental Material.

% ===============================================================================
%  METHODS CONVERGENCE
% ===============================================================================

Six established positive-density constructions detect the boundary independently. We illustrate with the truncated Wigner approximation~\cite{polkovnikov2010}, where the connection is most direct. TWA samples classical trajectories from initial conditions drawn from $W$, smoothed by a Gaussian of width $\sigma$ to obtain a positive sampling distribution. The standard prescription sets $\sigma = \Delta x$, the position spread of the state. At vacuum saturation $\Delta x = \sqrt{\hbar/(2m\omega)} = \delta x$, so the sampling width and the squeezed scale coincide and the choice is unremarkable. For $A \gg h/2$ the two diverge. The extent $\Delta x$ grows while $\delta x = \hbar/\Delta p$ shrinks. TWA still samples at width $\sigma = \Delta x$, but the resulting positive distribution cannot resolve features finer than the convolution of two such Gaussians with the Wigner function's own peak spacing. This sets the effective resolution at $\hbar/\Delta p$ along position. The sampling width chosen for positivity is the extent of the state. The resolution delivered is the reciprocal of the conjugate extent. This is the polar-dual scale.

Analogous derivations for generalized Husimi $P_{\lambda\theta}$~\cite{wodkiewicz1984}, vacuum Husimi $Q$~\cite{husimi1940}, homodyne tomography~\cite{lvovsky2009}, maximum-likelihood reconstruction, and Cahill--Glauber $W^{(s)}$~\cite{cahillglauber1969} appear in the Supplemental Material. Each method reaches the boundary by a different route. Table~\ref{tab:effective_resolution} summarizes.

At vacuum saturation $\Delta x = \delta x$ and the boundary is invisible. Methods, scales, and extents all coincide. For $A \gg h/2$ they separate. The extent $\Delta x$ grows, the polar-dual scale $\hbar/\Delta p$ shrinks, and each method's success regime tracks the latter. The methods are not converging on a normalization convention. They are converging on the boundary at which $W$ changes sign-character.

Two prior constructions reach a non-negative density by related routes. Dragoman~\cite{dragoman2005} weights the Wigner function of the state by the Wigner function of an external measuring apparatus. The non-negativity follows from the same trace inner-product identity used here, but the scale is the apparatus's, chosen by the experimenter, rather than the state's, and the boundary is not detected. Cordero, de Gosson, D\"orfler, and Nicola~\cite{cordero2018} study Zurek's diamond cat state in the Born--Jordan portrait, which is itself a smoothing of the Wigner distribution by a kernel with sinc structure on a phase-space scale fixed by the ordering. The Born--Jordan smoothing damps the sub-Planck peaks at a scale set by the ordering rule rather than the state. Both constructions smooth $W$ to obtain a smoother density. The kernel used here is the unique one with no free parameters, matched to the state's polar-dual structure, of fixed action $h/2$, and it resolves at the boundary the state itself defines.

The Wigner function remains the phase-space portrait of the state. Its sub-Planck dips quantify non-classicality~\cite{kenfack2004} and certify computational resources~\cite{marieisert2012,veitch2012}. They are visible on the fine side of the boundary, where they have always been. What is on the coarse side is the same peak structure read as a classical probability. The boundary itself is set by the action of the state and by nothing else. Wigner resolution is the reciprocal of action.
