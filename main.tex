\title{Uncertainty Principles and Finite Action in Phase Space}

\begin{abstract}
Quantum states occupy finite action cells in phase space. The uncertainty principles of Heisenberg, Robertson--Schr\"odinger, and de Gosson partition each cell into a conjugate pair of squeezed action areas, each bounded by $h/2$, totaling Planck's quantum of action $h$. Assuming sub-Planck pointwise measurements ignores this physical structure, producing semiclassical caustics and Wigner negativity. Probed at the resolution of its conjugate cells, the state is everywhere finite and everywhere non-negative.
\end{abstract}

% ======================================================================
% SLICE 1 --- PHYSICS
% ======================================================================

A quantum state occupies a finite region of phase space. The Heisenberg and Robertson--Schr"odinger uncertainty principles \cite{robertson1929,schrodinger1930},
\begin{equation}
\sigma_q \sigma_p \ge \hbar/2,
\label{eq:hup}
\end{equation}
forbid the state from being concentrated at a point. A point is a region of zero phase-space area, in direct violation of this bound.

\begin{figure}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/quantum_of_action.pdf}
\caption{\textbf{Conjugate structure of the quantum state} (schematic, 1D case). The Heisenberg limit $\sigma_q \sigma_p \ge \hbar/2$ admits semi-axes $\Delta q = \sqrt{2}\sigma_q$, $\Delta p = \sqrt{2}\sigma_p$; the state's $1\sigma$ contour is its Fermi blob \cite{degosson2012fermi}, of area $A = \pi\Delta q\Delta p \ge h/2$. Inside sit two conjugate quantum blobs \cite{degosson2009,degossoncharlyne2022}, $a_q = \pi\,\delta q\,\Delta p = h/2$ squeezed along position and $a_p = \pi\,\Delta q\,\delta p = h/2$ squeezed along momentum, summing to $h = a_q + a_p$. Their narrow widths are $\delta q = \hbar/\Delta p$ and $\delta p = \hbar/\Delta q$. As $A$ grows, $\Delta q$ and $\Delta p$ grow while $\delta q$ and $\delta p$ shrink: Eq.~(\ref{eq:hup}) on the blob and inside it.}
\label{fig:quantum_of_action}
\end{figure}

De Gosson identified the state's extent in phase space as the Fermi blob \cite{degosson2012fermi}, the elliptical region with semi-axes $\Delta q = \sqrt{2}\sigma_q$ and $\Delta p = \sqrt{2}\sigma_p$ and area
\begin{equation}
A = \pi \Delta q \Delta p \ge h/2,
\label{eq:fermi_blob_area}
\end{equation}
saturated by minimum-uncertainty Gaussians. A Fermi blob of area $A > h/2$ is not featureless. Its interior holds two conjugate quantum blobs \cite{degosson2009,degossoncharlyne2022},
\begin{equation}
a_q = \pi\,\delta q\,\Delta p = h/2, \qquad a_p = \pi\,\Delta q\,\delta p = h/2,
\label{eq:quantum_blobs}
\end{equation}
each carrying half the Planck quantum of action and together summing to $h$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:quantum_of_action}). The narrow widths
\begin{equation}
\delta q = \hbar/\Delta p, \qquad \delta p = \hbar/\Delta q
\label{eq:reciprocal}
\end{equation}
are reciprocal to the Fermi-blob semi-axes, fixed by the same Heisenberg constant in Eq.~(\ref{eq:hup}). As the action capacity $A$ grows, $\Delta q$ and $\Delta p$ grow and $\delta q$ and $\delta p$ shrink: the uncertainty principles bind the state at both scales. Gromov's non-squeezing theorem \cite{gromov1985} forbids any canonical transformation from compressing a quantum blob below $h/2$. The Planck quantum is the floor of phase space.

% ======================================================================
% SLICE 2 --- FORMALISM PROBLEMS
% ======================================================================

Two pathologies arise when formalisms probe the state at a point. The WKB density \cite{berry1977,langer1937}
\begin{equation}
|\psi_\text{WKB}(q)|^2 = \frac{A^2}{p(q)}\cos^2\!\left(S(q)/\hbar - \pi/4\right)
\label{eq:wkb}
\end{equation}
diverges at every classical turning point, where $p(q) \to 0$. The Wigner--Weyl distribution \cite{wigner1932}
\begin{equation}
W(q,p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \psi^*(q+y)\,\psi(q-y)\,e^{2ipy/\hbar}\, dy
\label{eq:wigner}
\end{equation}
takes negative values for any state beyond the ground state. Connection formulas and Airy uniformization \cite{langer1937,berry1977} patch the caustic locally; the Husimi $Q$-function \cite{husimi1940} smooths Wigner with a fixed coherent-state kernel of unit width and erases every scale below it. Each remedy treats one symptom in one formalism.

The two pathologies share a single root. WKB evaluates density at a precise $q$; Wigner evaluates it at an exact $(q,p)$. Each assumes a zero-area point measurement ($\delta q \to 0$ or $\delta p \to 0$), which by Eq.~(\ref{eq:reciprocal}) demands an unphysical, infinite conjugate spread ($\Delta p \to \infty$ or $\Delta q \to \infty$) and an unbounded action capacity ($A \to \infty$). The mathematical infinities of the caustics and the negativities of Wigner emerge precisely because the formalisms are driven into this forbidden limit.

% ======================================================================
% SLICE 3 --- PHYSICS
% ======================================================================

The two conjugate quantum blobs define the fundamental physical measurement limits of the state. The corresponding kernels of phase-space area $h/2$ are
\begin{equation}
G_{\delta q}(q,p) = \frac{1}{\pi}\exp\!\left(-\frac{q^2}{\delta q^2} - \frac{p^2}{\Delta p^2}\right),
\label{eq:Gdq}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
G_{\delta p}(q,p) = \frac{1}{\pi}\exp\!\left(-\frac{q^2}{\Delta q^2} - \frac{p^2}{\delta p^2}\right),
\label{eq:Gdp}
\end{equation}
$G_{\delta q}$ squeezed along position and $G_{\delta p}$ along momentum. Each saturates the symplectic-capacity bound at $h/2$.

For the classical microcanonical density $W_\text{cl}(q,p) \propto \delta(H - E)$ at the Bohr--Sommerfeld energy $E_n$, the orbit's time-averaged second moments give $\Delta q = \sqrt{2\langle (q-\langle q\rangle)^2\rangle_\text{orbit}}$ and $\Delta p = \sqrt{2\langle p^2\rangle_\text{orbit}}$. The marginal of the convolution over $p$ is
\begin{equation}
\rho_{\delta q}(q) = \int (W_\text{cl} * G_{\delta q})(q,p)\, dp,
\label{eq:rho_marginal}
\end{equation}
finite at the turning points where $|\psi_\text{WKB}|^2$ diverges and strictly positive everywhere because the kernel's Gaussian tails are. Figure~\ref{fig:semiclassical} shows three systems. The harmonic ground state collapses to a single Gaussian. The Morse oscillator at $n=8$ preserves the $n+1$ Bohr--Sommerfeld interference lobes; the WKB caustics become finite peaks. For the symmetric double-well sub-barrier doublet, per-well Langer--Airy constructions place artificial maxima at the inner turning points; $\rho_{\delta q}(q)$ is everywhere finite and seamlessly resolves the physical barrier penetration.

The same kernel applies in the wave formalism, with $\Delta q = \sqrt{2\langle (q-\langle q\rangle)^2\rangle_{\psi_n}}$ and $\Delta p = \sqrt{2\langle p^2\rangle_{\psi_n}}$ from the eigenstate's Robertson--Schr\"odinger covariance. The convolved Wigner density
\begin{equation}
P_{\delta q}(q,p) = (W * G_{\delta q})(q,p)
\label{eq:Pdq}
\end{equation}
is non-negative everywhere by Hudson's theorem \cite{hudson1974}, because $G_{\delta q}$ saturates the symplectic positivity condition. Figure~\ref{fig:wigner} shows the three systems as eigenstates. The Morse row at $n=8$ has $n+1$ alternating-sign Wigner lobes; $P_{\delta q}$ preserves the lobe envelope and is non-negative throughout, while Husimi smooths away the interior interference, leaving only two broad peaks at the classical turning points. The double-well antisymmetric partner $\psi_1$ has an exact wavefunction node at $q=0$, which coincides with $W_1(0,0) = -0.32$, the deepest point of the Wigner negativity --- the same unphysical point-measurement failing in two different formalisms. At resolution $\delta q$, $P_{\delta q}(q,0)$ is strictly positive throughout, including across the node and through the barrier where the Gaussian tails extend from each well.

A cat state has no classical orbit and no Bohr--Sommerfeld action \cite{schleich2001}. Its conjugate widths come from the wavefunction's covariance alone, with $\Delta q = \sqrt{2\langle (q-\langle q\rangle)^2\rangle_\psi}$ and $\Delta p = \sqrt{2\langle p^2\rangle_\psi}$. Figure~\ref{fig:cats} shows four cat configurations. Each row's Wigner cross-section dips below zero --- the central interference fringe of the 2-cat, the pair-fringe envelopes of the 3-cat, the chessboard of the axis-aligned compass, and the sub-Planck chessboard of the rotated compass. The chessboard scale Zurek identifies \cite{zurek2001} is the conjugate quantum-blob width $\delta q = \hbar/\Delta p$. At resolution $\delta q$, every cross-section is non-negative.

% ======================================================================
% SLICE 4 --- CLOSING
% ======================================================================

A quantum state is not a point in phase space and cannot be probed as one. Caustics and Wigner negativity are mathematical artifacts of forcing formalisms to assume otherwise. The physical reality of the state is governed by finite action, partitioned by the uncertainty principles into de Gosson's Fermi and conjugate quantum blobs. By respecting this geometric floor of phase space, the conjugate resolution guarantees the state remains everywhere finite and non-negative.
