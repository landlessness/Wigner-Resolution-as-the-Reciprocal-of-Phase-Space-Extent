\title{Wigner Resolution as the Reciprocal of Phase-Space Extent}

\begin{abstract}
The Heisenberg principle bounds a quantum state's outer extent in phase space. A complementary inner scale, set by the same quantum of action, is reached independently by two routes: de Gosson's symplectic polar duality of the covariance ellipsoid, and Zurek's interference-driven sub-Planck chessboard. Both arrive at $\delta x = \hbar/\Delta p$ and $\delta p = \hbar/\Delta x$ from the state's covariance. Convolution of the Wigner function with the squeezed-coherent kernel at this scale yields a non-negative density determined by the state itself. Established non-negative phase-space methods---truncated Wigner approximations, generalized Husimi smoothings, maximum-likelihood reconstructions, and Cahill--Glauber distributions---converge on this same scale in their respective regimes of success. We give the scale its symplectic-geometric origin.
\end{abstract}

The Wigner function~\cite{wigner1932} is the standard joint phase-space representation of a quantum state. For non-Gaussian states it takes negative values~\cite{hudson1974}. Many working contexts call for non-negative phase-space densities, and several construction routes have proven successful in practice. Truncated Wigner approximations~\cite{polkovnikov2010} sample classical trajectories and produce accurate semiclassical dynamics when the sampling matches the state's covariance. Husimi-style smoothings with state-dependent kernels~\cite{wodkiewicz1984, wuensche1995} reconstruct positive densities from generalized $P_{\lambda\theta}$ representations when $\lambda$ is chosen to match the state's spread. Maximum-likelihood reconstruction with Fock-state truncation produces non-negative reconstructions when the truncation is matched to the state's available action. Cahill--Glauber $W^{(s)}$ distributions~\cite{cahillglauber1969} recover positivity when the ordering parameter $s$ is chosen with respect to the state's covariance. Each method names its own kernel, its own ordering, or its own cutoff in the language of its subfield. The same scale appears in each: the reciprocal of the state's phase-space extent, $\hbar/\Delta p$ and $\hbar/\Delta x$. We give this scale its symplectic-geometric origin and derive the smoothing it defines.

% ===============================================================================
%  SLICE 1, PIECE B --- de Gosson
% ===============================================================================

A quantum state occupies phase space\footnote{Heisenberg cells are conventionally drawn as rectangles for visual simplicity; the correct symplectic-geometric object is an ellipsoid~\cite{degosson2013qblobs, degossoncharlyne2022}. Throughout this Letter, ``cell'' denotes the ellipsoid.}, with extent set by its covariance: an extended cell, the phase-space ellipsoid with semi-axes $\Delta x = \sqrt{2}\sigma_x$, $\Delta p = \sqrt{2}\sigma_p$ and action
\begin{equation}
A = \pi\,\Delta x\,\Delta p \ge h/2,
\label{eq:extended_cell}
\end{equation}
floored at $h/2$ by Heisenberg~\cite{robertson1929,schrodinger1930}. De Gosson finds a complementary cell inscribed in the extended one~\cite{degosson2013qblobs, degossoncharlyne2022}: the squeezed cell, of action exactly $h/2$,
\begin{equation}
a = \pi\,\delta x\,\delta p = h/2,
\label{eq:squeezed_cell}
\end{equation}
with semi-axes $\delta x$ and $\delta p$ tied by the polar-dual identity and $\delta x \le \Delta x$, $\delta p \le \Delta p$. Theorem~13 of \cite{degossoncharlyne2022} establishes that the inscribed cell carries the floor action $h/2$ exactly when the spreads it shows in $x$ and $p$ are reciprocals. Gromov's non-squeezing theorem~\cite{gromov1985} forbids any canonical compression below $h/2$.

% ===============================================================================
%  SLICE 1, PIECE C --- tomography
% ===============================================================================

The state fixes the extended cell; rotation through angle $\theta$ leaves it where it is. The inscribed squeezed cell is not so anchored: as $\theta$ varies, it pivots within the extended cell and its semi-axes flow according to
\begin{equation}
\delta x(\theta) = \hbar\sqrt{ \frac{\cos^2\theta}{\Delta x^2} + \frac{\sin^2\theta}{\Delta p^2} }, \qquad \delta p(\theta) = \frac{\hbar}{\delta x(\theta)},
\label{eq:cell_axes_theta}
\end{equation}
tied by the polar-dual identity~\cite{degossoncharlyne2022}. Shape tracks $\theta$ while the action stays at $h/2$. The cells $a_p$ and $a_x$ of Fig.~\ref{fig:zurek_cells} sit with axes along $x$ and $p$, at $\theta = 0$ and $\theta = \pi/2$; the rotated cell of Fig.~\ref{fig:de_gosson_cells} illustrates a typical orientation. The same rotated axis labels the quadrature in continuous-variable tomography~\cite{vogelrisken1989, lvovsky2009}.

% ===============================================================================
%  SLICE 1, PIECE D --- Zurek
% ===============================================================================
Zurek discovered that the Wigner function carries interference structure on sub-Planck scales~\cite{zurek2001}. With the state's extended cell $A$ providing the spreads $\Delta x$ and $\Delta p$, the smallest interference periods are
\begin{equation}
\delta x = \hbar/\Delta p, \qquad \delta p = \hbar/\Delta x.
\label{eq:zurek}
\end{equation}
These interference scales are the same $\delta x$ and $\delta p$ that bound de Gosson's squeezed cell when its axes lie along $x$ and $p$. Zurek shows that they control the state's sensitivity to phase-space displacements, and hence its decoherence rate.

% ===============================================================================
%  SLICE 1, PIECE E --- Convergence
% ===============================================================================
Two physical pictures---the cell that cannot be squeezed below the action $h/2$, and the chessboard of sub-Planck interference---reach the same scales. This convergence is the starting point of this Letter.

% ======================================================================
% SLICE 2 --- THE DENSITY
% ======================================================================

The Wigner--Weyl distribution~\cite{wigner1932}
\begin{equation}
W(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\int \psi^*(x+y)\,\psi(x-y)\,e^{2ipy/\hbar}\, dy
\label{eq:wigner}
\end{equation}
takes negative values for any non-Gaussian state~\cite{hudson1974}. By \eqref{eq:zurek} the pointwise limit $\delta x \to 0$ requires $\Delta p \to \infty$ and unbounded covariance area: pointwise sampling of $W$ asks a finer question than the uncertainty principle permits, and the negativity is the formalism's report.

The squeezed-coherent kernel matched to the state's covariance,
\begin{equation}
G_{\delta x}(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi}\exp\!\left(-\frac{x^2}{\delta x^2} - \frac{p^2}{\Delta p^2}\right),
\label{eq:Gdq}
\end{equation}
is the Wigner transform of the squeezed coherent state at the state's covariance~\cite{degossoncharlyne2022}, of area exactly $h/2$. The convolution
\begin{equation}
P_{\delta x}(x,p) = (W * G_{\delta x})(x,p)
\label{eq:Pdq}
\end{equation}
is non-negative everywhere, since convolution with any quantum-cell Wigner function yields a non-negative distribution~\cite{degosson2005bullsci,degosson2013qblobs}. The polar-dual partner $P_{\delta p} = W * G_{\delta p}$ resolves momentum at $\delta p$ symmetrically, with
\begin{equation}
G_{\delta p}(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi}\exp\!\left(-\frac{x^2}{\Delta x^2} - \frac{p^2}{\delta p^2}\right).
\label{eq:Gdp}
\end{equation}
The kernel is fixed by the state's covariance; no free parameter.

Figures~\ref{fig:eigen} and~\ref{fig:cats} show $P_{\delta x}$ for eight states with kernel set from each state's covariance, $\Delta x = \sqrt{2\langle (x-\langle x\rangle)^2\rangle}$, $\Delta p = \sqrt{2\langle p^2\rangle}$. The construction is non-negative across squeezed vacuum, harmonic eigenstates, Morse eigenstates with strong anharmonicity, double-well eigenstates with central-barrier interference, and four cat configurations.

The marginal $\int P_{\delta x}(x,p)\,dp$ equals $|\psi(x)|^2$ convolved with a one-dimensional Gaussian of width $\delta x$ and is therefore the smoothed-at-$\delta x$ true position distribution by construction. The same holds for $P_{\delta p}$ and $|\hat\psi(p)|^2$.

% ======================================================================
% SLICE 4 --- THE CLOSING
% ======================================================================

The polar-dual smoothing $P_{\delta x}$ produces a non-negative phase-space density at a resolution determined by the state itself. The resolution is geometric: it follows from de Gosson's symplectic polar duality and matches Zurek's interference scales. It coincides with the resolution at which established non-negative phase-space methods produce correct results.

The convergence across methods rooted in different premises---semiclassical sampling, ordering conventions, vacuum reference, Hilbert-space cutoffs---suggests that the polar-dual scale is a universal feature of phase-space resolution rather than an artifact of any particular construction. The geometric framework gives the convergence a unified origin in symplectic structure.
